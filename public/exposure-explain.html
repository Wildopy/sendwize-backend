Exposure by regulator
Sendwize tracks three regulators. Each one operates differently — ICO uses realistic case ranges, ASA uses reputational risk with referral likelihood, and CMA uses the statutory maximum only.
ICO ranges are based on published pre-DUAA enforcement decisions, narrowed by your revenue band. We look at what organisations your size were actually fined for each breach type — not the statutory maximum, which has almost never been applied at full value in PECR cases.

How "risk addressed" is calculated
riskAddressed = ∑ midpoint(realisticLow + realisticHigh) / 2
            for each completed ICO fix
The "comparable case risk addressed" figure on your dashboard is the sum of realistic midpoints for completed ICO fixes in the current month. It represents the centre of the range for what comparable organisations were fined in published decisions — not a prediction of what you would have been fined.

⚠️ DUAA 2025 — ranges will increase
The Data Use and Access Act 2025 significantly raised the ICO's maximum fine for PECR breaches to £17.5M or 4% of global annual turnover — whichever is higher. All ranges shown are based on pre-DUAA decisions. The first post-DUAA PECR enforcement decisions will be used to update the ranges as they are published. The current ranges are therefore conservative — actual future fines may be substantially higher.
The ASA cannot directly fine businesses. Instead, an upheld ruling is published permanently on asa.org.uk — searchable by name, indexed by search engines, and visible to customers, investors, and competitors. The financial risk comes from reputational damage and, for serious or repeat breaches, referral to Trading Standards under DMCCA 2024.
Referral risk by fix type
High referral risk
Misleading reference price
Fake 'was/now' pricing is a specific DMCCA 2024 enforcement target. The CMA has signalled active enforcement. High likelihood of Trading Standards referral for repeat or widespread breaches.
Medium referral risk
Fake urgency / Misleading claim
Common ASA complaint triggers. Deliberate or repeated misrepresentation increases referral risk significantly. Single first-time breaches usually result in an upheld ruling and compliance request only.
Low referral risk
Undisclosed ad
Failure to label marketing as advertising is a common first-time finding. Prompt remediation typically results in an upheld ruling and compliance request only — without Trading Standards referral.
If referred to Trading Standards — CMA penalties apply
When the ASA refers a case to Trading Standards under DMCCA 2024, it becomes a CMA matter. The CMA can impose fines directly without court proceedings — up to £300,000 or 10% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher. This is why medium and high ASA referral risk fixes surface a conditional CMA figure on your dashboard.
The CMA can impose fines directly without going to court — a significant change brought in by DMCCA 2024. The statutory maximum is the higher of £300,000 or 10% of global annual turnover. Co-operation discount
The CMA has indicated that businesses which co-operate promptly and demonstrate genuine remediation can negotiate settlement discounts. Consumer redress orders may be issued in addition to any fine — requiring direct compensation to affected customers. Fixing these issues before any investigation is substantially better than fixing them after contact from the CMA.
CMA enforcement decisions are published at gov.uk/cma. Sendwize will add realistic CMA ranges as post-DMCCA decisions are published.
What "not legal advice" means
Every exposure figure in Sendwize carries this label. Here is exactly what it means in practice.
✓
These figures are illustrative — based on published data
The ranges reflect what comparable organisations were actually fined. They are anchored on real decisions, not invented. Using them to understand and prioritise your compliance posture is exactly what they are designed for.
✗
These figures are not predictions of your fine
Many factors affect an actual enforcement outcome: your co-operation, the ICO's current enforcement priorities, the volume of contacts affected, whether the breach was deliberate, and your remediation speed. No software can predict these. A solicitor who specialises in data protection law can provide a proper assessment.
✗
These figures should not be used in financial reporting
Do not use Sendwize exposure figures as the basis for regulatory provisions in financial statements or investor documents without independent legal and financial advice.
