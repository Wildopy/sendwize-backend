List Intelligence — Methodology
How your list value
is calculated
Every figure shown in List Intelligence is derived from your actual contact data using a four-dimensional model. This page explains exactly what goes into the number — and why it decays over time.

Step 1 — The core formula
Every contact in your list gets a commercial value score. The formula is the same for everyone — what changes is the inputs, which come from your sector, your average order value, and that contact's engagement history.
commercialValue =
  conversionRate × averageOrderValue × sectorMultiplier × engagementMultiplier
This gives the expected revenue contribution of a single contact, adjusted for how likely they are to convert given their recent behaviour. Contacts with no engagement data use a conservative base multiplier of 0.5.
Step 2 — Sector conversion rates
The conversion rate is the starting assumption for what proportion of contacts in your sector will purchase from a given campaign. These are industry benchmarks — your actual rate may differ, but they give a consistent, comparable baseline.

The order multiplier accounts for typical deal sizes relative to the base AOV. Finance contacts tend to convert less often but at much higher value — so both factors are needed to reflect the real commercial picture.
Step 3 — Engagement multiplier
The base value is then adjusted upward or downward based on each contact's most recent engagement. A contact who purchased last month is worth significantly more than one who last opened an email two years ago.

Multipliers are applied to the base value from Step 2. A contact with no data defaults to 0.5× — below base — because the absence of engagement data is itself a signal of lower conversion likelihood.

This is repeated for every contact in your list. The total asset value shown on your dashboard is the sum of all active and recoverable contacts' commercial values.
Step 4 — Contact tiers
Contacts are then sorted into four tiers based on two dimensions: consent strength (which decays over time since signup) and deliverability score (derived from email address signals). Only active and recoverable contacts contribute to your list value figure.
Active
High consent, good deliverability. Full commercial value. Your core sendable list.
consent ≥60 · deliverability ≥60
Recoverable
Consent declining but above ICO threshold. Value is the intervention value — what you can recover with a re-engagement campaign.
consent 20–60 · deliverability ≥40
At risk
Above consent threshold but poor deliverability signals. Not included in list value — deliverability risk outweighs commercial potential.
consent ≥20 · deliverability <40
Liability
Below ICO consent threshold. Not included in list value. Generates a compliance fix and is added to your suppression registry.
consent <20
Consent strength decays exponentially from signup date — it halves every 12 months with no engagement. Purchases, clicks, and opens reset the decay clock, with purchases having the strongest reset effect.
Step 5 — Why the value decays over time
The unrealised value shown on your dashboard is not static. Each opportunity decays at a set rate per month — because the longer you wait to act, the lower the conversion likelihood becomes as engagement fades.

Each opportunity has its own decay rate stored at creation. Re-engagement opportunities decay at 2.5%/month. Priority send windows decay faster at 3%/month — because high-engagement windows close quickly. The value shown on your dashboard reflects today's decayed figure, not the original estimate.
Important: List value figures are estimates based on sector benchmark conversion rates and your stated average order value. They are not a guarantee of revenue. Actual results depend on campaign quality, send timing, offer relevance, and market conditions. Sendwize list value figures are intended as a comparative tool to prioritise contact segments — not as a financial projection. They should not be used as the basis for financial reporting or investor presentations without independent validation.
